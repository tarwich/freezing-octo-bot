Profile

Samuel is a highly versatile individual. Straddling over 20 different programming languages, he is able to handle challenges as they come. He is currently employed by Odyssey Communications Group as a lead software developer. His work at Odyssey includes the architecture, development, and deployment of a networked digital scoreboard system; the management of 23 application servers; and strong involvement in several web application software development projects.

Prior to working for Odyssey Communications Group, Samuel was a Command Control Communications and Computers Intelligence Surveillance and Reconnoissance Information Technology Project Manager. He oversaw the conversion of the entire aviation warning system and the complete weather information system from a 20-year-old technology to modern IP-based systems, allowing for access of the information from Command and Control centers throughout the Air Force Command. In addition to his job as a project manager, Samuel wrote a software application from the ground up to manage all air scheduling information and track the status of Air Force aircraft and equipment as well as record necessary maintenance information and preempt end of life of existing systems.

Samuel has been programming for quite some time. Interested in computer sciences, he entered classes at the age of eight and began programming in C++ as well as other languages. By the age of 11, he could program in C++, Basic, Visual Basic, and PERL. Soon after, he mastered assembly language and machine code, and he continued to study additional computer sciences throughout high school. At age 16, he worked at Nortel Networks in order to migrate the documentation division from the FrameMaker publishing system to an entirely SGML-based system. From SGML, the enterprise was able to easily export their documents to over 7 different types of media in one motion to include HTML, Word, and PDF.

Not wanting to be held back by the particulars of one operating system, he began learning web-based languages, which would allow him to take an application to a larger market. Now Samuel is looking for an opportunity to work with a team to develop an application that will revolutionize the way that people use computers. Samuel is certain that his skills and understanding of both low-level languages and network transport have honed him into a lethal weapon in the programming world.
